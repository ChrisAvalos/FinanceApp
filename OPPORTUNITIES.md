# Every money-on-the-table opportunity I can think of

A comprehensive catalog of (1) money you may already be owed and don't
know about, (2) money you can save through systems most people don't
exploit, and (3) creative angles to make this app meaningfully ahead
of every competitor.

Organized as: category → feature idea → implementation sketch →
estimated $-value to a typical person.

Date: 2026-04-27.

---

## 1. Money already owed to you (CLAIM)

These are dollars sitting in someone else's account waiting for you
to ask. Nobody has aggregated them in one place. **This is the
single biggest under-exploited category in personal finance.**

| Source | What | Avg claim | Discoverability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unclaimed property** | Old uncashed checks, forgotten utility deposits, dormant bank accounts, life insurance, employer 401k transfers gone wrong. NAUPA aggregates 50 state databases. | $80-200 (some $1k+) | MissingMoney.com / per-state portals |
| **IRS unclaimed refunds** | If you skipped filing one year and were owed money, the IRS holds it ~3 years before forfeit | Varies; ~$900 average | Where's My Refund tool |
| **State tax refunds** | Same shape, per state | $200-500 | State revenue dept site |
| **Old 401(k) plans** from past employers | Account still in plan you forgot about; rollover never completed | $5,000+ avg | National Registry of Unclaimed Retirement Benefits |
| **Old pensions** | Old defined-benefit plans, frozen but vested | $5,000+ avg | PBGC search |
| **Veterans benefits** | VA benefits not collected | Varies | VA.gov |
| **Treasury Direct savings bonds** | Old paper bonds that matured + weren't redeemed | $50-500 typical | TreasuryHunt.gov |
| **Property tax exemptions** | Homestead / senior / veteran / disability / energy exemptions you qualify for but don't claim | $300-2,000/yr | Local assessor's office |
| **Utility bill assistance** | LIHEAP energy assistance | $200-500/winter | Per-state |
| **Phone Lifeline / ACP** | Discounted phone service for income-eligible | $30/mo = $360/yr | LifelineSupport.org |
| **EITC underclaiming** | ~20% of EITC-eligible filers don't claim | $1,000-7,000 | IRS EITC eligibility |
| **Child Tax Credit underclaiming** | Especially for non-filers | $2,000-3,000/child | IRS |
| **IRA Energy Act rebates** | Heat pumps, EVs, induction stoves, weatherization | $500-9,000 | Per-state portals + IRS |
| **CFPB redress** | Consumer Financial Protection Bureau ordered companies to refund — database lookup | $50-500 | CFPB orders database |
| **State AG settlements** | State attorney general consumer fraud cases | $20-200 | Per-state AG office |
| **Manufacturer recalls with cash refunds** | Toy/car-seat/food/drug recalls with refund offer | $10-200 | Recalls.gov + brand-specific |
| **Airline DOT-mandated compensation** | Bumped, severely delayed, damaged baggage | $700-1,550 | DOT IDB / EU261 |
| **FDIC unclaimed deposits** | Bank failed, money still owed | Varies | FDIC.gov |
| **Class-action settlements** ✅ | Already shipped | $5-200 typical | TopClassActions / ClassAction.org |

**Total typical lifetime upside if all claimed: $10,000-50,000.**

## 5. Behavioral / system-design opportunities

What competitors don't do because it requires invention, not engineering:

1. **"Audit my financial life" wizard** — TurboTax-style flow that walks every category and identifies what you're missing. Surfaces opportunities ranked by ROI per minute.
2. **Local AI financial advisor (Ollama-driven)** — "How much did I spend on coffee in March?" "What's the best card for grocery?" "Why did my net worth drop $400 last week?" Already have the LLM plumbing.
3. **Action queue ranked by ROI/minute** — every opportunity becomes a task with $/minute estimate. "Spend 5 min searching MissingMoney.com → expected $150" is a much better hourly rate than 8 hours of overtime.
4. **Calendar-integrated expirations** — every offer expiry, statement close, claim deadline, sign-up-bonus minimum-spend deadline, free-trial end date — one calendar.
5. **Negotiation log + outcome tracking** — already have for retention. Extend to bills, medical, property tax. Build per-issuer playbooks that learn over time.
6. **Comparison shopping inside the app** — for a sub you currently use, show 3-5 alternatives with delta.
7. **Monte Carlo + what-if simulations** — what if I refinance / move / take a job / retire 5 years early?
8. **"Shadow self"** — what your wealth would look like if you'd made different choices N years ago.
9. **Wealth velocity tracker** — how fast is your net worth growing vs. the rate needed for your retirement target?
10. **FIRE calculator** — financial-independence retirement projection with savings-rate sensitivity.
11. **Lifestyle inflation detector** — "your spending grew 12% YoY but income only 5%."
12. **Geography arbitrage tool** — cost-of-living comparisons for relocation.
13. **Friction tax tracker** — "switching to annual billing on these 4 subs would save $312/yr."
14. **"Plug the leaks" dashboard** — every recurring outflow ranked: "is this earning its keep?"
15. **Marginal vs effective tax-rate displays** — most people don't know either.
16. **Debt avalanche/snowball animator** — visualize the payoff race.
17. **Compound-interest visualizer for the SAVING side**.
18. **Annual-fee math per card** — net rewards − fee per card, "should you cancel?"
19. **Unused card-benefit tracker** — TSA Precheck reimbursement, $300 airline credit, $200 hotel credit, lounge passes, free rebooking, statement credits, expiration dates.
20. **"You qualify for this" daily nudge** — wake up to "based on your spending, here's an offer / sub-cancel / claim worth $X today."
21. **Money-on-the-table dashboard** — single view aggregating all unclaimed claims, expiring offers, redeemable points, unrealized rewards.
22. **Spending heatmap** — calendar visual showing $-per-day pattern.
23. **Account-anniversary tracker** — for products with anniversary perks (Hyatt free night, anniversary IHG night).
24. **Card 5/24 rule tracker** — when can you apply for a new Chase card.
25. **Manufactured-spending opportunity finder** — rare advanced feature.
26. **Estate planning awareness** — "you don't have a will / your beneficiaries are stale" reminders.

## 6. Implementation priorities — most leverage first

Re-ordered by my read of "biggest single win for least dev work":

### Tier S — high value, high awareness deficit (build these first)

1. **Unclaimed property tracker** — generic table + per-state scraper or manual log. NAUPA / MissingMoney aggregator. Avg $80-200 per match, can be larger. **Most users don't know this exists.** ~3 days.
2. **Card sign-up bonus tracker** — table of welcome bonuses across cards, eligibility tracking (5/24 for Chase, lifetime-bonus rules), minimum-spend countdown. ~2 days.
3. **Unused card-benefit tracker** — yearly-resetting credits (Chase Sapphire Reserve $300 travel, Amex Plat $200 airline, etc.) with expiration warnings. ~2 days.
4. **HYSA / T-bill yield-arb suggestion** — surface "you have $X sitting in 0.01% checking; rolling to a 4.5% HYSA earns $Y/yr." Shows real-time top-3 rates. ~1 day for the suggestion engine; rate-fetch is the hard part.
5. **CFPB / state-AG redress search** — periodic cron that searches CFPB enforcement orders against the user's transaction history. ~3 days.
6. **Bill audit feature** — given a sub, fetch + display 2-3 cheaper alternatives. (Started for cell-phone in our existing rules.) ~3 days.
7. **Award-redemption value calculator** — given points balances + a reward target, show cents/point achieved across redemption methods. ~2 days.
8. **Tax-rate explainer** — given current filing status + income, show marginal + effective + bracket-fill remaining. ~½ day.
9. **HSA "save receipts, reimburse later" workflow** — receipt log with reimbursement-deferred date. Big long-term value. ~2 days.
10. **Money-on-the-table dashboard** — one panel that aggregates everything claimable / redeemable / expiring. ~2 days after individual features ship.

### Tier A — adds depth in places we already lead

11. **Subscription comparison shopping** — for a known sub, surface alternatives. ~3 days.
12. **Property tax appeal workflow** — for homeowners. ~3 days.
13. **Medical bill negotiation playbook** — extends retention pattern. ~2 days.
14. **Audit-my-financial-life wizard** — onboarding + periodic refresh. ~5 days.
15. **Anomaly detection** — large unusual transactions + statistical baselines. ~3 days.
16. **Free trial → paid alert** — N days before charge. ~½ day.
17. **Card 5/24 tracker** — new application eligibility. ~1 day.

### Tier B — bigger projects, build after S+A

18. **Local AI financial advisor (Ollama)** — extend the narrator pattern into a Q&A flow. ~1 week.
19. **Investment holdings + cost-basis** (from competitive doc). ~1-2 weeks.
20. **Tax-loss harvesting alerts**. ~3 days after #19.
21. **Retirement projection / FIRE calculator**. ~1 week.
22. **Real-estate via Zillow + Vehicle via KBB**. ~1 week total.

### Tier C — long-tail and creative

23. **Shadow self / opportunity-cost view**. ~3 days.
24. **Geography arbitrage**. ~3 days.
25. **Spending heatmap calendar**. ~1 day.
26. **Compound-interest visualizer**. ~1 day.
27. **Spending challenges / gamification**. ~3 days.
28. **Receipt-mining from email** — extract line items from order receipts in Gmail (we already parse). Cross-ref with recall lists. ~3 days.

## 7. Where this becomes "your personal CFO"

If we ship the catalog above, the app becomes:

- **Money-IN aggregator**: claims everything you're owed automatically (or surfaces it for your action)
- **Money-OUT optimizer**: tells you exactly which card / store / time / vendor is cheapest for each spend
- **Money-OPTIMIZE planner**: tax + retirement + investment + insurance + debt all in one
- **Money-PROTECT guardian**: catches fraud, expiring offers, unclaimed benefits, lapsing protections
- **Money-MAKE coach**: surfaces side-gig + bonus + yield opportunities

The pitch becomes:

> "Every other personal finance app shows you what already happened.
> This one tells you what to do next, ranked by dollars per minute of
> your time. It claims things on your behalf where it can, hands you
> the exact script where it can't, and never moves money without you."
